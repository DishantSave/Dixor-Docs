EmailNormalizer Reference

The EmailNormalizer is an internal service within the Dixor.Validation.Email library designed to transform email addresses into a canonical, consistent format.

Overview

Normalization ensures that two email addresses that are semantically identical (e.g., User@Example.com and User@example.com) are represented by the same string. This service focuses on safe, non-destructive transformations.

Normalization Steps

The Normalize method performs the following operations in order:

  1. Trimming: Removes any leading or trailing whitespace from the input string.
  2. Unicode Canonicalization: Applies NormalizationForm.FormC to ensure characters are represented in a standard, composed form.
  3. Domain Case Folding:
    • Identifies the domain portion (after the last @ symbol).
    • Converts the domain to lowercase (ToLowerInvariant).
    • Note: The local part is preserved as-is, as some email providers treat the local part as case-sensitive.
  4. Reconstruction: Concatenates the original local part and the newly normalized domain.
